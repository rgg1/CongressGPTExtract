                         OFFICE OF THE CHAPLAIN

     204A Hart Senate Office Building, phone 224-2510, fax 224-9686

    LLOYD JOHN OGILVIE, Chaplain of the U.S. Senate; born in Kenosha, 
WI, September 2, 1930; educated in the public schools of Kenosha; B.A., 
Lake Forest College; M.Th., Garrett Theological Seminary; D.D., 
Whitworth College; H.L.D., University of Redlands; D.H., Moravian 
College and Seminary; LL.D., Eastern College; New College, University of 
Edinburgh, Edinburgh, Scotland; student pastor, Gurnee Community Church, 
Gurnee, IL; pastor: Winnetka Presbyterian Church, Winnetka, IL (1956-
62); First Presbyterian Church, Bethlehem, PA (1962-72); First 
Presbyterian Church, Hollywood, CA (1972-95); Chaplain of the U.S. 
Senate (1995); media communicator, author, and frequent speaker 
throughout the nation; awards: Distinguished Service Citation, Lake 
Forest College; Preacher of the Year Award and Angel Award (Religion in 
Media); Silver Angel Award, 1982, 1986; Gold Medallion Book Award, 1985; 
William Booth Award, 1992; married to Mary Jane Jenkins-Ogilvie; three 
children: Heather, Scott, and Andrew; author of 44 books.

                     OFFICE OF THE SERGEANT AT ARMS

             S-321 The Capitol, phone 224-2341, fax 224-7690

    JAMES W. ZIGLAR, Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, U.S. Senate; 
elected on October 15, 1998. Born in Pascagoula, Mississippi; graduated 
from George Washington University, Washington, DC, B.A. and J.D. 1964-
71, Aide to Senator James O. Eastland (D MS); 1971-72, Legislative and 
Public Affairs Officer, Dept. of Justice; 1972, Law Clerk to U.S. 
Supreme Court Associate Justice Harry A. Blackmun; 1973-77, Associate, 
Mudge, Rose, Guthrie, Alexander and Ferdon law firm, New York, NY; 1977-
80, Partner, O'Connor, Cavanagh, Anderson, Westover, Killingsworth and 
Beshears law firm, Phoenix, AZ; 1980-84, Senior V.P., Dillon, Read and 
Co., Inc.; 1984-87 and 1990-88, Managing Director, Paine Webber, Inc.; 
1987-88, Asst. Secy. for Water and Science, Dept. of the Inteiror, 1989, 
Managing Director, Drexel Burnham Lambert, Inc.; 1998-present, current 
position. Admitted to VA Bar, 1972; DC Bar 1973; NY Bar, 1975; AZ Bar, 
1977. Awards: joint recipient (with Sen. Malcolm Wallop) Water Statesman 
of the Year Award, Natl. Water Resources Association, 1988.
