    LAWRENCE H. SUMMERS, Secretary of the Treasury; born in New Haven, 
CT, in 1954; education: S.B., Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 
1975; Ph.D., Harvard University, 1982; professional: Domestic Policy 
Economist, President's Council of Economic Advisers, 1982-1983; 
Professor, Harvard University, 1983-1993; became the youngest tenured 
professor in the University's history; Vice President of Development 
Economics, and Chief Economist, at the World Bank, 1991-1993; Under 
Secretary of the Treasury for International Affairs, 1993-1995; Deputy 
Secretary of the Treasury, 1995-1999; nominated by President Clinton, 
and confirmed by the U.S. Senate, as Secretary of the Treasury, 1999; 
awards: John Bates Clark Medal (outstanding economist); National Science 
Foundation's Alan Waterman Award (outstanding scientific achievement); 
member, Econometric Society; and the American Academy of Arts and 
Sciences; married to Victoria Summers; they have three children; twin 
daughters and a son.
