    DIRK KEMPTHORNE, Secretary of the Interior; born in San Diego, CA, 
October 29, 1951; education: B.S., University of Idaho, 1975; 
professional: Executive Assistant to the Director, Idaho Department of 
Public Lands, 1976-78; Executive Vice President, Idaho Home Builders 
Association, 1978-81; campaign manager for gubernatorial candidate Phil 
Batt, 1982; State Public Affairs Manager, FMC Corporation, 1983-86; 
Major of Boise, Idaho, 1986-93; Senator, Idaho, 1993-99; Governor, 
Idaho, 1999-2006; Chairman, National Governors Association, 2003; 
appointed to the National Assessment Governing Board; appointed to the 
Homeland Security Task Force; President, Council of State Governments; 
Chairman, Western Governors Association; served on Executive Committees 
of the National Governors Association and Republican Governors 
Association; religion: Methodist; family: married to Patricia; children: 
Heather and Jeff; nominated by President George W. Bush to become the 
49th Secretary of the Interior, and was confirmed by the U.S. Senate on 
May 26, 2006.
