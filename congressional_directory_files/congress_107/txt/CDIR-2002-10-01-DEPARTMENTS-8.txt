    ELAINE L. CHAO, Secretary of Labor; education: B.A., Mount Holyoke 
College, 1975; M.B.A., Harvard University, 1979; she also studied at the 
Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Dartmouth College, and Columbia 
University; employment: Citicorp, 1979-1983; BankAmerica Capital Markets 
Group, 1984-1986; Distinguished Fellow, Heritage Foundation, 1996-2001; 
public service: White House Fellow, Office of Policy Development, 1983-
1984; Deputy Maritime Administrator, Department of Transportation, 1986-
1988; Chairwoman, Federal Maritime Commission, 1988-1989; Deputy 
Secretary of Transportation, 1989-1991; Peace Corps Director, 1991-1992; 
President and Chief Executive Officer of the United Way of America, 
1992-1996; family: married to U.S. Senator Mitch McConnell (R-KY); 
recipient of many awards for her community service and professional 
accomplishments; and recipient of 11 honorary doctorate degrees from 
numerous colleges and universities; nominated by President George W. 
Bush to become the 24th Secretary of Labor, and was confirmed by the 
U.S. Senate on January 29, 2001.
