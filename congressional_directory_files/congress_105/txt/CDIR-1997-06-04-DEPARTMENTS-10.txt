    ALEXIS M. HERMAN, of Mobile, AL, Secretary of Labor; graduated form 
Xavier University, New Orleans, LA in 1969; began her career as a social 
worker for Catholic Charities, developing training opportunities for 
unemployed youth, unskilled workers and new entrants to the Mobile labor 
force at Ingall's Shipbuilding, Inc., in Pascagoula, MS; in the 1980s, 
founded A.M. Herman and Associates, a human resources consulting firm 
with corporate and government clients; director of the Women's Bureau, 
U.S. Department of Labor, during the Carter administration; chief 
executive officer of the 1992 Democratic National Convention Committee; 
deputy director of the Presidential Transition Office, 1992; assistant 
to President Clinton and director of the White House Public Liaison 
Office; confirmed as the twenty-third Secretary of Labor by the U.S. 
Senate, May 2, 1997.
