    RAY LaHOOD, Secretary of Transportation; born in Peoria, IL, 
December 6, 1945; education: B.A., Bradley University, Peoria, IL; 
professional: United States Congressman from 1995-2009; member of the 
House Committees on Transportation and Infrastructure; Veterans Affairs, 
and Agriculture, Intelligence, and Appropriations; chairman of four 
Bipartisan Congressional retreats; Chief of Staff to U.S. Congressman 
Robert Michel, 1983-94; Illinois State Representative from 1982-83; 
District Administrative Assistant to Congressman Thomas Rails, 1977-82; 
Bi-State Metropolitan Planning Commission, 1974-77; teacher; director, 
Rock Island County Youth Services Bureau, 1972-74; family: married to 
Kathy LaHood; four children: Darin, Amy, Sam, and Sara; nine 
grandchildren; nominated by President Barack H. Obama to become the 16th 
Secretary of Transportation, and was confirmed by the U.S. Senate on 
January 23, 2009.
